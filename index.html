Eclipses

What are Eclipses?

Eclipses are when one celestial body moves into the shadow of another celestial body, causing a temporary dimming or blocking of the light. In the context of Earth, we mostly refer to solar and lunar eclipses.

Solar Eclipses

Total

Moon completely covers the Sun

Partial

the Moon covers only a portion of the Sun

Hybrid

transitions between an annular and a total eclipse along its path

Annular

Moon appears smaller than the Sun

Lunar Eclipses

Total

Earth completely covers the Moon

Partial

Earth causes a subtle shading on the Moon's surface

Penumbral

transitions between an annular and a total eclipse along its path
