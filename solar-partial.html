Partial Eclipse



How It Happens:

In a partial solar eclipse, the Moon covers only a portion of the Sun's visible disk.

Causes:

Similar to a total solar eclipse, but observers are in the penumbral shadow of the Moon, where only part of the Sun is obscured.

Mechanics:

Depending on the observer's location, they see a crescent-shaped Sun as the Moon passes in front of it. The extent of the partial eclipse varies based on geographic location.
